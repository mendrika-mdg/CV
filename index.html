About

I am a PhD researcher in Machine Learning at the University of Leeds, working on deep learning methods for satellite-based convective storm nowcasting across Africa.

My research focuses on object-based artificial intelligence approaches for probabilistic nowcasting using geostationary satellite observations, with a strong emphasis on operational forecasting, interpretability, and early warning systems.

I am part of the NAIAR project (Nowcasting with AI of African Rainfall), collaborating with the University of Leeds, UKCEH, NCAS, and international forecasting partners.

Featured Project — PANCAST

PANCAST is an operational AI-based probabilistic nowcasting system designed to predict convective storm occurrence across Africa using satellite observations and deep learning.

The framework combines object-based storm representations with spatio-temporal deep learning architectures to generate real-time probabilistic nowcasts of severe convection.

PANCAST is being operationalised in collaboration with UKCEH and international partners to support forecasting and early warning applications across Africa.
